Research Profile

My Ph.D. research studies CFET as a circuit-technology design space rather than only a device-scaling concept. The central theme is device-circuit co-optimization: layout, tier setting, and sizing are used to evaluate how CFET vertical integration can improve SRAM-level PPA under practical design constraints.

Research

Ph.D. Research Directions

01

Device-Circuit DTCO for CFETs

Building a device-to-circuit optimization view of CFET technology, where device architecture, layout rules, parasitic loading, and circuit metrics are evaluated together.

02

SRAM-Centered Density-to-PPA Translation

Studying whether CFET vertical integration can become useful SRAM PPA gain after accounting for stability, access delay, energy, routing, and array-level overhead.

03

Layout and Sizing Optimization

Exploring CFET SRAM layout and transistor sizing choices through circuit-facing metrics while keeping unpublished design details private.

04

Constraint-Aware CFET Evaluation

Evaluating emerging CFET circuit options under practical design, layout, and technology constraints without treating isolated device density as the only success metric.
